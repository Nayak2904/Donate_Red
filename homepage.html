IMPORTANCE

Blood donation is a critical and life-saving act that plays a fundamental role in healthcare systems worldwide. By donating blood, individuals have the power to save lives and support patients in various medical emergencies, surgeries, accidents, and chronic illnesses. The availability of safe and compatible blood is essential for treating patients with blood disorders, managing chronic conditions, and providing support during natural disasters and pandemics.
Beyond its immediate life-saving impact, blood donation promotes public health, fosters community solidarity, and contributes to emergency preparedness. It is a humanitarian act that embodies compassion, empathy, and altruism, enabling individuals to make a tangible difference in the lives of others and build healthier and more resilient communities.

Blood groups

Blood groups, also known as blood types, are classifications based on the presence or absence of specific antigens on the surface of red blood cells. The most well-known blood group systems are the ABO system and the Rh system.
The ABO blood group system is one of the most well-known and widely used blood group classifications in medicine. It categorizes blood into four main groups: A, B, AB, and O. These blood groups are determined by the presence or absence of two antigens, known as A and B antigens, on the surface of red blood cells.

The ABO system
There are 4 main blood groups defined by the ABO system:

  1. Blood group A – has A antigens on the red blood cells with anti-B antibodies in the plasma.
  2. Blood group B – has B antigens with anti-A antibodies in the plasma.
  3. Blood group O – has no antigens, but both anti-A and anti-B antibodies in the plasma.
  4. Blood group AB – has both A and B antigens, but no antibodies.
  5. Blood group O is the most common blood group. Almost half of the UK population (48%) has blood group O.
Receiving blood from the wrong ABO group can be life-threatening. For example, if someone with group B blood is given group A blood, their anti-A antibodies will attack the group A cells..
